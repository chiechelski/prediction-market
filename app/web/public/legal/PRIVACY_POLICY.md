## 2. Scope

- **Solana validators, RPC nodes, indexers, block explorers** — they process transactions according to their own terms and network rules.

## 3. How prediction-market and DeFi interfaces differ from centralized exchanges

Regulated prediction-market operators (for example, **CFTC-designated contract markets** in the U.S.) often collect extensive **know-your-customer (KYC)** data, banking details, and trade history under a **member agreement**.  

This Interface is **non-custodial**: we do **not** take custody of your private keys or digital assets, and we may **not** collect the same categories of personal data as a centralized exchange. Much of what you do is recorded **on a public ledger** under your **wallet address**, not in our databases.

## 4. Information we may collect

### 4.1 You provide directly

- **Correspondence:** email address, name, or other details if you contact support or subscribe to updates.
- **Feedback or surveys** (if offered).

We do **not** require an email or password to **connect a wallet** and use core Interface features.

### 4.2 Automatically collected (Interface and infrastructure)

Depending on how we operate the site, we may collect:

- **Technical data:** IP address, approximate location derived from IP, browser type and version, device type, operating system, referral URL, timestamps.
- **Usage data:** pages viewed, clicks, session duration, errors and performance metrics.
- **Identifiers:** cookie IDs or similar, **unless** you disable cookies where technically possible.

### 4.3 Local storage on your device

The Interface may store **preferences** in your browser (e.g., selected Solana cluster, custom RPC endpoints, **local market labels** you choose). This data stays on your device unless you clear site data or we sync it (we describe syncing below if applicable).

### 4.4 Blockchain data (public, not “ours”)

When you sign transactions:

- Your **wallet public key**, transaction data, token balances, program accounts, and market state are **visible on Solana** to anyone.
- Third parties can **index, analyze, and display** this information without our involvement.

**Wallet addresses are pseudonymous** but may become associated with you if you link them to identity elsewhere (e.g., social media, centralized exchange withdrawals).

### 4.5 Wallet connection

Wallet extensions communicate with the Interface per the wallet provider’s rules. We may receive your **public key** and transaction **signing requests** you approve. We do **not** receive your **private keys** or seed phrase through standard wallet adapters.

### 4.6 Information from third parties

We may receive **aggregated or technical** data from analytics, hosting, security, or anti-abuse vendors, consistent with their policies and our agreements.
